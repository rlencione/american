Safety Minute

For today‚Äôs Safety Minute, I want to talk about Ground to Flight Deck Communication. As we are all aware Aircraft movement is a critical part of the operation and having investigated damage to aircraft and equipment during aircraft movement. Ground to flight deck teamwork requires effective communication, When engaging in ground to flight deck communication use the C's. Best Practices to Follow: 1. Communicate: Clearly communicate to the flight deck and get confirmation. If there is any question whether there is a misunderstanding, verify. 2. Coordinate: Be sure your action(s) are coordinated with the flight deck as necessary to ensure effective movement of the aircraft. 3. Clear and Concise: When communicating, be sure you're clear and concise in order to avoid miscommunication, Injury to others, or damage to aircraft and equipment. 4. Critical: If you are unsure about your path you've reached a critical point of movement, use the S.T.O.P approach (Stop Think Observe Proceed). It is always better to stop and realign if needed.

Safety Minute

Do you have Emergency Procedures for the elevators in your building? If you work in an older building is your elevator up to code? For today's safety minute I'd like to discuss Elevator Safety. There recently wasn an injury within Base Maintenance due to an elevator being stuck between floors. Panic set in and the employee tried to pry the doors open injuring their shoulder trying to escape. It's important all team members know what to do if youfind yourself in this situation without a cell phone or two-way communication: *First press the alarm button to get the attention of others that the elevator has stopped. *Stay calm and try to not panic and wait until assistances in on their way *Do not try to pry open the doors and climb out. ASME A17.1 Safety Code for elevators reads: two - way communication means between the car and a location staffed by authorized personnel shall be provided. Door restrictors are devices that prevent passengers from opening the elevator doors if the elevator stops between floors. Tragic accidents can occur when passengers attempt to exit the car and leap to the landing. Passengers need to stay in the elevator until trained personnel arrive to remove them safetly. The elevator maintenance service companies have the sole responsibility of maintaining and servicing the elevators in a safe and efficient manner. Scheduled inspections, tests, and quick response on repairs are specificed in the service contracts
