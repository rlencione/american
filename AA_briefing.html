For todayâ€™s Safety Minute, I want to talk about fuel spills. Recently my station was involved in a known material spill (jet fuel) on the ramp outside of the maintenance hangar. While accomplishing aircraft defuel operations, it was noticed by maintenance personnel that fuel was pouring out of the top of the fueling truck, the defuel operation was halted and local management was notified. The local fire department was called and determined that the total amount of fuel lost was less than 20 gallons. Our maintenance people quickly gathered supplies from the spill kit located inside the hangar and, using proper PPE, contained the spill, preventing any contamination into the local storm drains. The clean-up materials were stored in an appropriate waste container and local management contacted the Department Environmental Coordinator for assistance with the containers. Reference your local spill response plan and the American Airlines Environmental Management Manual (EMM) sections 03.01- 03.08 for guidelines on how to respond to spills. As per EMM section 03.01, it is the policy of American Airlines to respond to spills in an efficient and economic manner, while ensuring the safety of its employees, passengers, facilities and the surrounding environment. The quick actions by maintenance and local management shared in the story above prove that having a spill response plan in place prevented employee injury, aircraft/equipment damage and prevented any adverse impact to the local environment.

Safety Minute

Do you have Emergency Procedures for the elevators in your building? If you work in an older building is your elevator up to code? For today's safety minute I'd like to discuss Elevator Safety. There recently wasn an injury within Base Maintenance due to an elevator being stuck between floors. When the elevator stopped the employee opened the emergency phone to call security to find that the phone had been removed. Panic set in and the employee tried to pry the doors open injuring their shoulder trying to escape. It's important all team members know what to do if youfind yourself in this situation without a cell phone or two-way communication: *First press the alarm button to get the attention of others that the elevator has stopped. *Stay calm and try to not panic and wait until assistances in on their way *Do not try to pry open the doors and climb out. ASME A17.1 Safety Code for elevators reads: two - way communication means between the car and a location staffed by authorized personnel shall be provided. Door restrictors are devices that prevent passengers from opening the elevator doors if the elevator stops between floors. Tragic accidents can occur when passengers attempt to exit the car and leap to the landing. Passengers need to stay in the elevator until trained personnel arrive to remove them safetly. The elevator maintenance service companies have the sole responsibility of maintaining and servicing the elevators in a safe and efficient manner. Scheduled inspections, tests, and quick response on repairs are specificed in the service contracts.
